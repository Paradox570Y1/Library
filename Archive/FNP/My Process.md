# Round 2 : Interview

I had two technical interview rounds. In both rounds, the interviewer had my OA sheet from the initial assessment, and the discussions were conducted in a pen-and-paper format.
As per the company’s process, there were supposed to be two additional rounds—Technical Interview 3 and an HR Interview. However, I was fortunate to be selected after the second technical round itself, and the HR round was skipped for everyone due to time constraints.

## Technical Interview 1

 This interview begin by interviewer randomly picking an MCQ from my OA sheet to discuss, in my case, I was asked to explain why the time complexity of AVL Trees is nlogn for n operations. After that i was given a database design problem. I was asked to create a schema to manage records of students arriving at the university by car, followed by discussion based questions on how the schema would evolve if a student owned multiple cars or if multiple students carpooled in the same vehicle. This discussion lasted about 10–15 minutes and focused on design clarity. Next, I solved two DSA problems on paper: first, finding the second minimum element in an array, with a follow up to explain the approach to find the kth minimum element, and second, writing the optimal solution to check whether a target exists in a rotated sorted array or not. The round concluded with a brief informal discussion about my family background and hobbies.

# Technical Interview 2

This interview round began with a discussion on CPU scheduling algorithms, including Round Robin, SJF, and SRTF, which I simulated on paper to explain their working. The conversation then moved to system design concepts, where I was asked about load balancers and deadlocks. I explained deadlocks by drawing a Resource Allocation Graph (RAG) and discussed strategies for their prevention, eventually steering the discussion toward topological sorting for dependency resolution. Later, the interviewer asked about my preferred programming language, which was Java in my case, and followed up with questions on interfaces, how they differ from abstract classes, checked vs. unchecked exceptions with examples, and runtime polymorphism. The round concluded after this discussion.
