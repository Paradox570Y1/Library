# WHAT WILL YOU DO SECTION

The highlighted line is about **researching a feature/problem, collaborating with peers, defining a clear goal, and validating it with a mentor**. To answer the recruiter confidently, you’ll want to show that you can follow a **structured yet simple framework** that demonstrates problem-solving, teamwork, and mentorship alignment.

Here’s a framework + example workflow you can use:
## 📌 Framework: **R-D-C-V**

1. **Research** – Understand the feature/problem through exploration, reading documentation, competitor analysis, or small prototypes.
    
2. **Discuss** – Share findings with teammates, brainstorm solutions, and refine understanding.
    
3. **Convert to Goal** – Translate the problem into a clear, achievable internship goal (measurable, time-bound).
    
4. **Validate with Mentor** – Present the refined goal to your mentor for feedback and alignment with business needs.

## 📌 Example Workflow (Problem Statement → Goal)

**Problem Statement:**  
_"Improving the collaboration experience on Adobe Document Cloud by making it easier to add comments on mobile devices."_

**Step 1 – Research:**

- Study how commenting currently works in Adobe Acrobat mobile.
- Analyze competitor apps (Google Docs, Notion) for mobile annotation UX.
- Identify user pain points (e.g., “too many taps to add a comment”).

**Step 2 – Discuss with Team:**

- Share insights in group discussion.
- Brainstorm ideas like: voice-to-text comments, swipe gestures for quick notes, or sticky comment buttons.
- Narrow down to one or two promising solutions.

**Step 3 – Define as a Goal:**

- Goal: _“Design and implement a prototype of a quick-gesture mobile commenting feature in Adobe Document Cloud within the internship period.”_

**Step 4 – Validate with Mentor:**

- Present the research + proposed goal to mentor.
- Refine scope based on mentor’s input (e.g., focus only on Android first, or only on voice comments).

**Final Outcome:**

- A concrete, validated project that can be delivered in 10 weeks.
- Clear teamwork and mentor involvement.

### Introduction: The Adobe Business Model

Adobe has successfully transitioned from a seller of perpetual software licenses to a **subscription-based (SaaS) model** across all its units. This creates predictable, recurring revenue and deeply embedded customer relationships. Its financial performance is typically reported in two main segments:

1. **Digital Media Segment:** Encompassing Creative Cloud and Document Cloud.
    
2. **Digital Experience Segment:** Encompassing the Experience Cloud.
    

The "Cloud Technology" and "Operations" are cost centers (R&D and Infrastructure) that enable these revenue-generating segments.

---

### 1. Adobe’s Digital Media Business Unit (Creative Cloud)

This is Adobe's **cash cow** and the foundation of its modern success.

- **Financial Performance:** This is Adobe's largest revenue segment.
    
    - In fiscal year 2023, the Digital Media segment brought in **$14.21 billion**, representing about 71% of Adobe's total revenue.
        
    - It has incredibly high gross margins (around 87-88%) due to the software-as-a-service (SaaS) model. The cost of delivering software to one more user is virtually zero.
        
    - It boasts a massive, loyal user base of millions of creative professionals and enthusiasts who rely on these tools for their livelihoods, creating immense stickiness.
        
- **Specialty & Strategic Importance:**
    
    - **Industry Standard Monopoly:** Products like Photoshop, Illustrator, and Premiere Pro are so entrenched in creative workflows that they are synonymous with their categories. This creates a powerful moat.
        
    - **The Ecosystem Lock-in:** Files are often created in layered, proprietary formats (`.psd`, `.ai`, `.aep`) that work best within the Adobe ecosystem. Switching to a competitor involves massive workflow disruption and file conversion headaches.
        
    - **Continuous Innovation:** The subscription model funds constant updates with new AI-powered features (like **Generative Fill** in Photoshop), ensuring the products stay ahead of competitors.
        

---

### 2. Adobe’s Digital Experience Business Unit (Experience Cloud)

This is Adobe's **major growth engine** and its bet on the future of enterprise software.

- **Financial Performance:** This is Adobe's second-largest, high-growth segment.
    
    - In fiscal year 2023, the Digital Experience segment generated **$5.86 billion**, representing about 29% of total revenue.
        
    - While smaller than Digital Media, it often grows at a faster rate and is critical for Adobe's long-term expansion into the massive enterprise software market.
        
    - It operates in a highly competitive space with players like Salesforce, Oracle, and SAP.
        
- **Specialty & Strategic Importance:**
    
    - **The Complete Suite:** Adobe's key differentiator is offering a fully integrated stack—from analytics (Adobe Analytics) and content creation (Experience Manager) to personalization (Target) and marketing automation (Marketo). This "one-stop-shop" approach is highly valuable to large enterprises tired of piecing together solutions from different vendors.
        
    - **Data is the Key:** The crown jewel of this unit is the **Adobe Experience Platform (AEP)**. It ingests vast amounts of customer data, which is then used to power every other application in the suite. This data moat is incredibly valuable.
        
    - **High-Value B2B Customers:** It sells to large enterprises with big budgets, leading to large Average Contract Values (ACV) and deep, strategic partnerships.
        

---

### 3. Adobe Document Services (Document Cloud)

Document Cloud is a star performer within the larger Digital Media segment and is a market leader in its own right.

- **Financial Performance:** Document Cloud revenue is reported under the broader Digital Media segment. However, Adobe often breaks out its growth.
    
    - It is a multi-billion dollar business on its own, with revenue exceeding **$2.5 billion** annually.
        
    - It demonstrates very strong, consistent growth, frequently outpacing the growth of the Creative Cloud sub-segment.
        
- **Specialty & Strategic Importance:**
    
    - **The PDF Standard:** Adobe literally invented the PDF. This gives it unparalleled authority and trust in the document space. Acrobat and Reader are ubiquitous.
        
    - **Strategic Bridge Product:** Adobe Sign and Acrobat are used by _everyone_—from students and consumers to the largest legal and financial enterprises. This makes Document Cloud a fantastic entry point into organizations that may later adopt the full Experience Cloud suite.
        
    - **Massive Total Addressable Market (TAM):** Nearly every knowledge worker on the planet interacts with PDFs and documents, giving Adobe a huge market to sell into.
        

---

### 4. Cloud Operations & 5. Cloud Technology

These are not revenue-generating product categories but are the critical **enabling forces** behind all of Adobe's services.

- **Financial Performance:** These are cost centers. The expenses for running massive data centers, developing the core platform, and ensuring security and reliability are baked into the R&D and Cost of Revenue lines on Adobe's income statement. This investment is non-negotiable and is what allows the other segments to be so profitable.
    
- **Specialty & Strategic Importance:**
    
    - **Scale and Reliability:** Cloud Operations runs one of the largest and most reliable SaaS infrastructures in the world, serving billions of transactions and storing exabytes of customer data. Their specialty is delivering a "always-on" experience globally.
        
    - **Unified Foundation - Adobe Experience Platform (AEP):** This is the single most important piece of technology in Adobe's portfolio.
        
        - **Specialty:** It is a truly enterprise-grade, real-time data platform. It can ingest data from any source (websites, apps, CRM systems, point-of-sale), unify it into a single customer profile, and make it instantly available to any other application (Analytics, Target, CDP).
            
        - **Real-Life Usage:** When you look at a product on your phone and then see an ad for it later on your laptop, that cross-device recognition is powered by AEP.
            
    - **AI & Sensei:** **Adobe Sensei** is the AI and machine learning framework that is integrated across all clouds. It's not a product you buy, but the technology that powers features like:
        
        - **Creative Cloud:** Auto-reframe in Premiere Pro, Content-Aware Fill in Photoshop.
            
        - **Experience Cloud:** Predictive analytics for which customer is most likely to churn.
            
        - **Document Cloud:** Automated form field recognition and text extraction in Acrobat.
