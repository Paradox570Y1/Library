##### 📉 Q: Tell me about a time you failed at something and what you learned.

**Answer:**
**S (Situation):**  
At the end of my 4th semester, I got a chance to participate in a placement drive by **CodeNation** at the University. The Coding test included four coding problems to be solved in two hours.

**T (Task):**  
My goal was to solve at least three questions, as they seemed manageable after reading through them which took me 15 minutes.

**A (Action):**  
I began confidently, but spent almost 45 minutes solving just the first problem. By the time I moved to the second one, I only had limited time left. I managed to almost completed the implementation of logic, but unfortunately, the test ended before I could finish. I wasn't shortlisted, and I realized that although my problem-solving skills were solid, my **time management** during coding rounds was lacking.

**R (Result):**  
This experience was a turning point. I started focusing more on **timed practice** by participating in contests on platforms like LeetCode and Codeforces. I learned that it’s not just about solving problems—it’s about solving them efficiently under pressure. Since then, I’ve seen significant improvement in both my speed and accuracy, and I feel much more confident in future coding assessments.
