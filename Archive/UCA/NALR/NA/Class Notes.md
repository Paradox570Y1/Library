- To find the greatest number that divides 187, 233, and 279, leaving the same remainder in each case, compute the differences between the numbers:
```

 233 - 187 = 46
 279 - 233 = 46
 279 - 187 = 92
The required divisor d must divide these differences, as the same remainder implies that the differences are divisible by d. Thus, d must be a common divisor of 46, 46, and 92.

The greatest common divisor (GCD) of 46 and 92 is 46, since 92 = 2 × 46. Therefore, the greatest such dd is 46.

Verify with the numbers:

- 187 ÷ 46 = 4 (since 46 × 4 = 184), remainder = 187 - 184 = 3
- 233 ÷ 46 = 5 (since 46 × 5 = 230), remainder = 233 - 230 = 3
- 279 ÷ 46 = 6 (since 46 × 6 = 276), remainder = 279 - 276 = 3

All leave the same remainder of 3, confirming that 46 works.
```

- To find a hint  to find hcf of two numbers take their difference  , usually that difference is multiple of GCD.
