### ✅ **Meaning of "Redundancy"**

**Redundancy** means having **more than what is needed**, often to provide **backup** or due to **repetition**. It can have **slightly different meanings depending on the context**.

---

### 🔍 **General Definition:**

> **Redundancy** refers to something that is **unnecessary or duplicated**, or it can mean having **extra components for safety** or **reliability**.


### 📚 **Examples by Context:**

|**Context**|**Meaning of Redundancy**|
|---|---|
|**Everyday Use**|Saying or doing the same thing again unnecessarily.  <br>📌 _"Saying 'free gift' is redundant — all gifts are free."_|
|**Technology/Computing**|**Backup systems** in case one part fails.  <br>📌 _"Servers have redundancy to avoid downtime during failures."_|
|**Job/Employment (UK/India)**|When a job position is **no longer needed**, and the person is let go.  <br>📌 _"He lost his job due to redundancy."_|
|**Engineering**|Extra components built in to **improve reliability**.  <br>📌 _"Airplanes have redundant systems for safety."_|

---

### 🧠 **In Simple Words:**

- **Unneeded repetition** → bad redundancy (e.g., "revert back" = revert is enough)
- **Backup for safety** → good redundancy (e.g., backup generator)
---

### ✅ **In Short:**

> **Redundancy** means **extra or repeated** elements — either **unnecessary** or **intentional for safety/reliability**.
