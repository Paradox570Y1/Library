**Preening** is a verb that means:

1. **(For birds)**: To **clean, arrange, or smooth** their feathers using their beak.
2. **(For people, figuratively)**: To **admire oneself**, **groom carefully**, or act **proudly or vainly**.

---

### 🔹 **Examples in Sentences:**

#### 🐦 **Literal (Birds):**

- _The parrot sat on the branch, **preening** its feathers calmly._
- _Birds **preen** to keep their feathers clean and waterproof._

#### 💁‍♂️ **Figurative (Humans):**

- _He stood in front of the mirror, **preening** before the big date._
    
- _She was **preening** with pride after receiving the award._
    
- _Stop **preening** like you're the center of the universe!_  
    → _(Used to tease someone acting overly proud)_
    

---

### 🔹 **Synonyms:**

|For Birds|For People|
|---|---|
|Groom|Show off|
|Clean|Flaunt|
|Arrange|Admire oneself|
|Straighten (feathers)|Strut / Pose / Be vain|

---

### 🔹 **Memory Tip:**

> Imagine a **peacock preening** — both fixing its feathers **and** showing off. That’s the dual meaning!
