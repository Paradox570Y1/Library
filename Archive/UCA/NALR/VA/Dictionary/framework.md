### ✅ **Meaning of "Framework"**

The word **"framework"** refers to a **basic structure or supporting system** that helps organize, guide, or support something more complex.

---

### 🔍 **General Definition:**

> A **framework** is a **structured plan or system** that provides **guidance**, **support**, or **rules** for building, understanding, or managing something.

---

### 📚 **Examples by Context:**

|**Context**|**What "Framework" Means Here**|
|---|---|
|**General Use**|A basic **outline** or **structure** of a plan or idea.  <br>📌 _"The new policy is built within the framework of national goals."_|
|**Software/Programming**|A **collection of pre-written code, tools, and libraries** to help developers build applications faster and more efficiently.  <br>📌 _"React is a JavaScript framework for building user interfaces."_|
|**Education**|A **curriculum structure** showing what subjects or skills should be taught.  <br>📌 _"The education framework emphasizes critical thinking."_|
|**Legal/Political**|A **set of principles or rules** that define how a system (like a constitution or law) works.  <br>📌 _"The legal framework protects the rights of citizens."_|

---

### 🧠 **Visual Example (Programming)**:

Think of a **framework** like the **foundation and frame of a house**:

- You still design the rooms (your code).
    
- But the **walls, plumbing, and structure** are already there (provided by the framework).
    

---

### ✅ **In Short:**

> A **framework** is a ready-made **structure** or **guide** that helps you **build, organize, or understand** something more effectively.
