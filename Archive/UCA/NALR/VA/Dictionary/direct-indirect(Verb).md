## 1. **Definitions**


### 🔹 **Direct Verbs**

- **Definition:** A **direct verb structure** involves a **verb acting directly** on an **object** (called a **direct object**).
- Spoken in actual words of speaker
- **Structure:**  
    **Subject + Verb + Direct Object**
    
    > e.g., _She sent **a letter**._

### 🔹 **Indirect Verbs**

- **Definition:** An **indirect verb structure** adds a second object—the **indirect object**, which indicates **to whom** or **for whom** the action is performed.
- Spoken in your own words.
- **Structure (2 types):**  
    **Subject + Verb + Indirect Object + Direct Object**  
    → _She sent **him** a letter._  
    **Subject + Verb + Direct Object + to/for + Indirect Object**  
    → _She sent **a letter to him**._
    

---

## 2. **Conversion Rules**

### 🔄 **Direct ➡️ Indirect**

**Rule 1:** Identify the **direct object**.

> _She gave **a gift**._

**Rule 2:** Decide **to whom** or **for whom** the action was done (indirect object).

> _She gave **a gift to John**._

**Rule 3:** Reorder to insert the indirect object **before** the direct object (without using "to"/"for"):

> _She gave **John a gift**._

✅ _Final Indirect Form:_

> She gave John a gift.

---

### 🔄 **Indirect ➡️ Direct**

**Rule 1:** Identify the **indirect object** and **direct object**.

> _She gave **John** a gift._

**Rule 2:** Place the **direct object** after the verb.

> _She gave **a gift**_

**Rule 3:** Add “**to**” or “**for**” before the indirect object.

> _She gave a gift **to John**_.

✅ _Final Direct Form:_

> She gave a gift to John.

---

## 3. **Examples**

### 🔁 **Direct to Indirect**

#### ✳️ Example 1:

- **Direct:** The teacher explained the lesson **to the students**.
    
- **Indirect:** The teacher explained **the students** the lesson.
    

#### ✳️ Example 2:

- **Direct:** She made a cake **for her friend**.
    
- **Indirect:** She made **her friend** a cake.
    

---

### 🔁 **Indirect to Direct**

#### ✳️ Example 1:

- **Indirect:** He told **me** a story.
    
- **Direct:** He told a story **to me**.
    

#### ✳️ Example 2:

- **Indirect:** They bought **her** a gift.
    
- **Direct:** They bought a gift **for her**.
