### **Meaning of "Federal" in General**

The term **"federal"** refers to a **system of governance** in which **power is divided between a central (national) authority and smaller political units**, such as states or provinces. This system balances powers so that both levels of government have some autonomy.

---

### **1. What Does “Federal” Mean in General?**

- **Federal = relating to a union of states under a central government.**
    
- In a **federal system**, **multiple regions (states or provinces)** share control with a **central government**, but they also **maintain their own laws and systems** to some extent.
    

🧠 **Example**:  
India and the United States are **federal countries** — they have both a central (national) government and state governments with their own responsibilities.

---

### **2. Federal Government**

- **Federal government** refers to the **national government** in a federal system.
    
- It is the **central authority** that governs the entire country but shares power with regional or state governments.
    

🧠 **Example**:  
In India:

- **Federal Government** = Central Government in New Delhi
    
- **State Governments** = Punjab Government, Kerala Government, etc.
    

In the U.S.:

- **Federal Government** = U.S. Government in Washington D.C.
    
- **State Governments** = California Government, Texas Government, etc.
    

---

### **3. Federal Information**

- "Federal information" means **any data, policies, or content** that comes from or is controlled by the **federal (central) government**.
    

🧠 **Examples**:

- **Federal law** = A law passed by the national government.
    
- **Federal data** = Census data or national statistics collected by the government.
    
- **Federal agency** = A national body (like FBI in the U.S. or CBI in India).
    

---
