### 🔹 **Definition:**

**Serendipity** is the **occurrence of something good or beneficial by chance**, often in a **happy or unexpected way**.

> It's when you **find something valuable, pleasant, or useful without actually looking for it**.

---

### 🔹 **Origin:**

- Coined by **Horace Walpole** in 1754.
    
- Based on the old tale of “**The Three Princes of Serendip**” (Serendip = ancient name for Sri Lanka), whose heroes always made discoveries by **accident and sagacity**.
    

---

### 🔹 **Examples in Sentences:**

1. **Finding love unexpectedly:**
    
    > _It was pure **serendipity** that they met on a delayed train._
    
2. **Discovering something while doing something else:**
    
    > _He found the perfect book in a shop he entered by mistake — a real case of **serendipity**._
    
3. **Scientific discovery:**
    
    > _Penicillin was discovered by **serendipity** when Alexander Fleming noticed mold killing bacteria._
    

---

### 🔹 **Synonyms:**

- Happy accident
- Fluke
- Chance discovery
- Blessing in disguise

---

### 🔹 **Opposites (Antonyms):**

- Misfortune
- Bad luck
- Accident (without benefit)
---

### 🔹 **Quick Memory Tip:**

> **Serendipity** = **Serenity + Discovery**  
> (A peaceful or lucky surprise discovery)
