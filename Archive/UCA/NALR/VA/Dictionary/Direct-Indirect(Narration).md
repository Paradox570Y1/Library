### 1. **Tense**: Present Indefinite (Simple Present)

 **Direct Speech Example**:

> He said, "I play cricket every Sunday."

 **Conversion to Indirect Speech**:

- **Reporting verb**: "said" (in the past) → this means we must shift the tense of the reported speech.
- **Tense rule**: Present Indefinite → Past Indefinite.
- "play" changes to "played"
- Pronoun "I" becomes "he" (to match the subject of the reporting verb).
- Time expressions (like "today", "tomorrow", etc.) also change, if applicable—but none here.

 ##### **Indirect Speech Example**:

> He said that he played cricket every Sunday.


### 1. **Tense**: Present Continuous

**Direct Speech Example**:

> She said, "I am reading a book."

**Conversion to Indirect Speech**:

- Reporting verb "said" is in the past → the tense of the reported speech must shift.
- **Tense rule**: Present Continuous → Past Continuous.
- "am reading" becomes "was reading"
- Pronoun "I" becomes "she" (to match the subject of the reporting verb).

**Indirect Speech Example**:

> She said that she was reading a book.


### 1. **Tense**: Present Perfect

**Direct Speech Example**:

> He said, "I have finished my homework."

**Conversion to Indirect Speech**:

- Reporting verb "said" is in the past → shift the tense of the reported clause.
- **Tense rule**: Present Perfect → Past Perfect.
- "have finished" becomes "had finished"
- Pronoun "I" becomes "he", and "my" becomes "his".

**Indirect Speech Example**:

> He said that he had finished his homework.


### 1. **Tense**: Present Perfect Continuous

 **Direct Speech Example**:

> She said, "I have been working on this project."

 **Conversion to Indirect Speech**:

- Reporting verb "said" is in the past → tense must shift.
- **Tense rule**: Present Perfect Continuous → Past Perfect Continuous.
- "have been working" becomes "had been working"
- Pronoun "I" changes to "she"

 **Indirect Speech Example**:

> She said that she had been working on that project.



### 1. **Tense**: Past Indefinite (Simple Past)

**Direct Speech Example**:

> He said, "I watched the movie yesterday."

**Conversion to Indirect Speech**:

- Reporting verb "said" is in the past → tense must shift.
- **Tense rule**: Past Indefinite → Past Perfect.
- "watched" becomes "had watched"
- "I" changes to "he"
- Time expression "yesterday" becomes "the previous day" or "the day before"

**Indirect Speech Example**:

> He said that he had watched the movie the day before.


### 1. **Tense**: Past Continuous

**Direct Speech Example**:

> She said, "I was cooking dinner."

**Conversion to Indirect Speech**:

- Reporting verb "said" is in the past → tense may shift.
- **Tense rule**: Past Continuous → Past Perfect Continuous.
- "was cooking" becomes "had been cooking"
- "I" changes to "she"

**Indirect Speech Example**:

> She said that she had been cooking dinner.


### 1. **Tense**: Past Perfect

**Direct Speech Example**:

> He said, "I had completed the assignment."

**Conversion to Indirect Speech**:

- Reporting verb "said" is in the past, but **Past Perfect** already indicates a completed past action.
- **Tense rule**: **Past Perfect remains unchanged**.
- Only pronouns or time expressions may change.
- "I" becomes "he"

 **Indirect Speech Example**:

> He said that he had completed the assignment.


### 1. **Tense**: Past Perfect Continuous

 **Direct Speech Example**:

> She said, "I had been studying for three hours."

**Conversion to Indirect Speech**:

- Reporting verb is in the past, and the **Past Perfect Continuous** tense is already the furthest back in the timeline.
- **Tense rule**: **No change** to Past Perfect Continuous.
- Only pronouns or time references might change.
- "I" becomes "she"

**Indirect Speech Example**:

> She said that she had been studying for three hours.



### 1. **Tense**: Future Indefinite (Simple Future)

**Direct Speech Example**:

> He said, "I will go to the market."

 **Conversion to Indirect Speech**:

- Reporting verb is in the past → future tense changes accordingly.
- **Tense rule**: **"will" becomes "would"**
- "I" becomes "he"

**Indirect Speech Example**:

> He said that he would go to the market.



### 1. **Tense**: Future Continuous

**Direct Speech Example**:

> She said, "I will be attending the meeting."

 **Conversion to Indirect Speech**:

- Reporting verb is in the past → future tense needs to shift.
- **Tense rule**: **"will be" becomes "would be"**
- "I" becomes "she"

**Indirect Speech Example**:

> She said that she would be attending the meeting.


### 1. **Tense**: Future Perfect

**Direct Speech Example**:

> He said, "I will have completed the project by Monday."

**Conversion to Indirect Speech**:

- Reporting verb is in the past → future perfect shifts accordingly.
- **Tense rule**: **"will have" becomes "would have"**
- "I" becomes "he"

**Indirect Speech Example**:

> He said that he would have completed the project by Monday.



### 1. **Tense**: Future Perfect Continuous

**Direct Speech Example**:

> She said, "I will have been working here for five years next month."

 **Conversion to Indirect Speech**:

- Reporting verb is in the past → future perfect continuous changes.
- **Tense rule**: **"will have been" becomes "would have been"**
- "I" becomes "she"

*Indirect Speech Example**:

> She said that she would have been working there for five years the following month.

## 🔁 **Indirect to Direct Speech – Summary**

### 🧭 **General Steps**:

1. **Identify the reporting verb** (e.g., _said, told, asked_).
    
2. **Change the verb tense** **backwards** (reverse of indirect conversion).
    
3. **Change pronouns** back to match the original speaker/listener.
    
4. **Restore original time/place words** (e.g., _that day → today_).
    
5. **Use quotation marks** to show the exact words spoken.
