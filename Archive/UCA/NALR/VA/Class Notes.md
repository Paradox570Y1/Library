## 📌 Vocabulary & Meaning Clusters

### 1. **Desire and Temptation**

- **Temptation** / **Desire** / **Want** / **Longing** / **Craving**  
    → All represent a strong feeling of wanting something.
    

---

### 2. **Signs and Symbols**

- **Sign** / **Signal** / **Symbol**  
    → Used to indicate or represent something.  
    ❗ _Note_: Do **not** confuse with **signature** — which refers to a person's name written in a distinctive way.
    

---

### 3. **Deception and Tricks**

- **Lure** / **Trap** / **Deception** / **Deceive** / **Dodge**  
    → All imply some form of misleading or tricking someone.
    

---

## 📌 Grammar & Usage

### 4. **Correct Usage of "In Spite Of"**

❌ _Incorrect:_

> In spite my hard work, he failed me.

✅ _Correct:_

> In **spite of** my hard work, he failed me.  
> ✅ (✔) _Correct usage of preposition "of" after "in spite"._

### 6. **Modal Verbs**

**Modal verbs** are **helping (auxiliary) verbs** used with the **main verb** to express abilities, possibilities, permissions, obligations, or advice.

- **Should** / **Ought to**  
    → Express duty, advice, or probability.

| Modal Verb   | Function                        | Example Sentence                          |
| ------------ | ------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------- |
| **Can**      | Ability / Permission            | She **can** swim. / **Can** I come in?    |
| **Could**    | Past ability / Polite request   | I **could** run fast when I was younger.  |
| **May**      | Possibility / Formal permission | It **may** rain. / **May** I leave early? |
| **Might**    | Weak possibility                | He **might** come later.                  |
| **Shall**    | Suggestion / Formal future (UK) | **Shall** we begin?                       |
| **Should**   | Advice / Recommendation         | You **should** eat healthy.               |
| **Will**     | Future certainty                | I **will** call you tomorrow.             |
| **Would**    | Hypothetical / Politeness       | I **would** love to join you.             |
| **Must**     | Strong obligation / Necessity   | You **must** wear a seatbelt.             |
| **Ought to** | Moral duty / Advice             | You **ought to** respect your elders.     |

- What is voice?
	- There are two types of voices:
		- active voice: Where subject is to be made prominent (whatever written in tense)
		- passive voice: Where object is to be made prominent.

Example:
>Whenever we write passive voice verb's 3rd form is used always with slight change.
- I teach aptitude. (active)
	- Aptitude is taught by me. (passive)

>When doer is unknow or missing use passive voice
- Someone stole my wallet. (active)
	- My wallet was stolen by someone. (passive)

> When subject is meant to do something like police catches thieves then use passive.
> But in case a Jeweler catches a thief then you can use active as it's not jeweler's responsibility to catch thieves.
- The police have caught the thief. (active) 
	- The thief has been caught by the Police. (passive)
