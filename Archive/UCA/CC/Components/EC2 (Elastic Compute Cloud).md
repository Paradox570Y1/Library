Amazon **EC2 (Elastic Compute Cloud)** is a core service of Amazon Web Services (AWS) that provides on‑demand, elastic, and scalable computing power. In simpler terms, think of it as renting virtual servers (“instances”) in the cloud. You can choose the specifications (CPU, memory, storage), launch or stop them at any time, and pay only for what you use .

- **Beta launch**: The limited public beta of EC2 began on **August 25, 2006**, offering virtual machines in the AWS “us-east-1” (Northern Virginia) region [techcrunch.com+2en.wikipedia.org+2en.wikipedia.org+2](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timeline_of_Amazon_Web_Services?utm_source=chatgpt.com).

### 📌 Key Features

- **Elasticity**: Easily scale up or down based on workloads.
- **Pay-as-you-go**: Charges are only for the compute time you consume.
- **Variety of configurations**: Different “instance types” tailored to tasks—from CPU-heavy to GPU-intensive jobs [medium.com](https://medium.com/%40servifyspheresolutions/what-is-amazon-ec2-5e21dcc53bb1?utm_source=chatgpt.com)[fr.wikipedia.org+5docs.aws.amazon.com+5medium.com+5](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/AWSEC2/latest/UserGuide/concepts.html?utm_source=chatgpt.com).
    
- **Integration**: Works seamlessly with AWS services like EBS (storage), Auto Scaling, VPC, and security groups [en.wikipedia.org+4docs.aws.amazon.com+4geeksforgeeks.org+4](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/AWSEC2/latest/UserGuide/concepts.html?utm_source=chatgpt.com).

### 📜 Why It Matters

EC2 launched what we now know as Infrastructure-as-a-Service (IaaS), transforming how companies deploy and manage servers. It shifted the [[paradigm]] from owning physical hardware to renting scalable compute resources that can be provisioned instantly .
