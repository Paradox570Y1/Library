# ATS
- Used by top 100 companies.
- This system is highly un-emotional. It doesn't care for your name , story.
- The top header section of CV is called kill zone or dead zone.
- **Never** use Abbreviations in your CV.
- There is no place for special characters in ATS unless you are using it them in particular use cases like email uses @. Other such symbols are (; ,+,-,comma).
- Don't create any tables while feeding your education
- ATS shortlists the CV based on first 40% section of the CV.
- ATS doesn't check for bold, italics or underlined words. It doesn't matter.
- In kill zone just under your name put the role that the company is asking as per the JD. As your name doesn't determine anything.
- Don't write any Objectives. Stick to conventional headers that recruiters stick to.
- ATS parse speed is 1/60th of second.
- Only use "SANS SERIF" font family like (Ariel, Calibri, Times New Roman).
- Spacing  should be between 1.15 - 1.5.
- Font size should be 12 to 14.
- Make your CV compliant to city.
- Find out all JD's of targeted company from last 5 to 10 years from job boards like LinkedIn, Google jobs, Glass Door etc. Lets say we found 50 JD's.
- Find out all the JD's based on ecosystem of that company as per location. Lets say we found 70 JD's
- Now take intersection of JD's from both sphere. Look for personality skills and other skills they want. Find those keywords.
- Then look for positioning gradient and D.V.D (Dollar Value Denomination).
- After that we again go back to those JD's and find out the positioning of these keywords. Now based on our JD move the skills asked to the top as per Scarcity distribution but also keep other skills.
- After the skills populate the CV with rest of the keywords.
- Links like LinkedIn should be kept in parenthesis bcz moment ATS sees parenthesis it opens that in another page.
