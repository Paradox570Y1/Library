While the everyday words “confidence” and “self‑confidence” are often used interchangeably, there is a subtle distinction:

1. **Confidence**
    
    - **Definition:** A general feeling of certainty or trust in someone or something.
        
    - **Scope:** Can refer to belief in external factors or other people (e.g., “I have confidence in my team,” or “I’m confident the plan will work”).
        
    - **Source:** Often comes from evidence, past results, or the proven competence of others or systems.
        
    - **Example:** You might feel confident that a bridge is safe because engineers designed and tested it rigorously.
        
2. **Self‑confidence**
    
    - **Definition:** A belief in your own abilities, judgment, and worth.
        
    - **Scope:** Entirely inward—how much you trust yourself to perform, decide, and persevere.
        
    - **Source:** Built over time through personal achievements, positive self‑talk, mastery experiences, and resilience in the face of setbacks.
        
    - **Example:** You feel self‑confident when you trust your public‑speaking skills enough to deliver a presentation without excessive nerves.
        

---

|Aspect|Confidence|Self‑confidence|
|---|---|---|
|**Object**|People, plans, systems, ideas|Yourself|
|**Basis**|Evidence, track record, expertise|Self‑awareness, past successes, mindset|
|**Emotional tone**|Assurance in outcome or others|Assurance in one’s own capability|
|**Development**|Grows by seeing reliable results|Grows by personal practice and reflection|

---

### Why It Matters

- **Balanced confidence** helps you rely on both external support and your own strengths.
    
- **Strong self‑confidence** empowers you to take on challenges, rebound from failure, and set ambitious goals.
    

### Tips to Boost Self‑Confidence

1. **Celebrate Small Wins:** Track your progress on tasks, however minor, and acknowledge your role in them.
    
2. **Skill Building:** Deliberately practice areas you find challenging—mastery breeds confidence.
    
3. **Positive Self‑Talk:** Replace self‑doubt (“I can’t…”) with constructive affirmations (“I can learn to…”).
    
4. **Visualization:** Mentally rehearse success in specific situations (e.g., interviews, presentations).
    
5. **Seek Feedback:** Constructive input helps you correct course and reinforces growth.
