## 🔹 1. **Waterfall Model**

### 🔸 Description:

A linear, sequential model where each phase (Requirement → Design → Implementation → Testing → Deployment → Maintenance) must be completed before moving to the next.

### ✅ Best for:

- Projects with **well-defined requirements**.
- Short-term projects with **clear goals and no expected changes**.
- **Government or military** projects.

### ❌ Not ideal for:

- Projects with evolving requirements.
- High risk or complex software.

---

## 🔹 2. **V-Model (Validation and Verification Model)**

### 🔸 Description:

An extension of the Waterfall model, but with a focus on testing at each development stage.

### ✅ Best for:

- Safety-critical systems (e.g., **medical devices, avionics**).
- Projects where **testing is a priority**.

### ❌ Not ideal for:

- Projects with frequently changing requirements.

---

## 🔹 3. **Incremental Model**

### 🔸 Description:

Software is built and delivered in small, functional pieces (increments). Each increment adds functionality.

### ✅ Best for:

- Projects that need **quick releases**.
- When the **core features are needed first**, and extras can come later.

### ❌ Not ideal for:

- Systems requiring **tight integration of all components from the beginning**.

---

## 🔹 4. **Spiral Model**

### 🔸 Description:

Combines iterative development with risk analysis. Each loop (spiral) represents a phase of the process.

### ✅ Best for:

- **Large, complex, and high-risk** projects.
- When **risks need to be actively managed**.

### ❌ Not ideal for:

- Small projects due to overhead.

---

## 🔹 5. **Agile Model**

### 🔸 Description:

An iterative and collaborative model with continuous feedback and flexible changes. Examples: Scrum, Kanban, XP.

### ✅ Best for:

- Projects with **changing requirements**.
- Teams that work closely with the client.
- **Startups, SaaS products, modern web/mobile apps.**

### ❌ Not ideal for:

- Projects requiring **strict documentation or fixed scope** from the beginning.

---

## 🔹 6. **RAD (Rapid Application Development)**

### 🔸 Description:

Emphasizes rapid prototyping and fast feedback. User involvement is key.

### ✅ Best for:

- Projects that need to be **delivered quickly**.
- Projects with **available user feedback** and **modular structure**.

### ❌ Not ideal for:

- Large systems or **projects without active user involvement**.

---

## 🔹 7. **Prototype Model**

### 🔸 Description:

Builds a **working prototype** early, then refines it based on user feedback.

### ✅ Best for:

- Projects where **requirements are unclear**.
- Applications that need **high user interaction** (like UI/UX-heavy apps).

### ❌ Not ideal for:

- Systems with **stable and well-understood requirements**.

---

## 🔹 8. **Big Bang Model**

### 🔸 Description:

No formal planning. Developers start coding with little or no understanding of requirements.

### ✅ Best for:

- **Small projects or academic experiments**.
- When the scope is **unclear and flexibility is required**.

### ❌ Not ideal for:

- Any **large, structured or critical system**.

---

## ✅ Summary Table:

| Model           | Best for                                       | Avoid when...                                     |
| --------------- | ---------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------- |
| **Waterfall**   | Clear requirements, no changes expected        | Requirements are unclear or likely to change      |
| **V-Model**     | High-reliability, testing-focused systems      | Project needs agility or evolving scope           |
| **Incremental** | Quick delivery of functional parts             | Requires full system integration early            |
| **Spiral**      | High-risk, large-scale systems                 | Small, low-budget projects                        |
| **Agile**       | Dynamic, collaborative, fast-evolving projects | Needs strict scope and full documentation upfront |
| **RAD**         | Rapid delivery with user input                 | Large or technically complex systems              |
| **Prototype**   | Unclear requirements or UI-heavy               | Clear requirements exist from the start           |
| **Big Bang**    | Small, informal projects or exploration        | Mission-critical, large-scale applications        |
|                 |                                                |                                                   |
