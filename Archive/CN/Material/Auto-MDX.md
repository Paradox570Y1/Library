Auto-MDIX (Automatic Medium-Dependent Interface Crossover) is ==a feature on network devices that automatically detects the type of Ethernet cable (straight-through or crossover) connected to a port and configures the port to work correctly, eliminating the need for specific cable types for certain connections==. Essentially, it allows you to use either type of cable to connect to other devices, and the interface will figure out the correct configuration. 

Here's a more detailed explanation:

- **Traditional Ethernet Cabling:**
    
    In the past, connecting two devices required specific cable types. Connecting two devices of the same type (like two switches) required a crossover cable, while connecting two devices of different types (like a switch and a computer) required a straight-through cable. 
    

- **Auto-MDIX Simplifies:**
    
    With auto-MDIX, the switch port automatically senses whether a straight-through or crossover cable is used and adjusts its transmit and receive pins accordingly. 
    

- **No More Cable Confusion:**
    
    This means you can use either a straight-through or crossover cable to connect devices without worrying about whether you have the right cable type. 
    

- **Default Setting:**
    
    Auto-MDIX is typically enabled by default on modern network devices like Ethernet switches.
