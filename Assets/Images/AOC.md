Case Study: Qualcomm and Stellantis

Customer Challenge:

Stellantis, a global automaker, aimed to equip all 14 of its diverse vehicle brands with a uniform, scalable, and future-proof digital cockpit and 5G telematics platform. The goal was to provide a consistent and continually upgradeable user experience, streamline the supply chain, and enable new connected services across its entire lineup, from entry-level to luxury vehicles.

Qualcomm's Solution: Snapdragon Digital Chassis

Qualcomm provided its comprehensive Snapdragon Digital Chassis platform, a modular and scalable suite of connected vehicle technologies. This includes: 

Snapdragon Cockpit Platforms: For infotainment and in-car experiences, supporting advanced graphics, multi-display setups, and AI-powered features.

Snapdragon Auto Connectivity Platforms: Providing high-speed 4G/5G, Wi-Fi, and Bluetooth connectivity for telematics and cloud services.

Snapdragon Car-to-Cloud Services: Enabling over-the-air (OTA) updates and a platform for new, monetizable services and features on demand throughout the vehicle's lifespan. 

Outcomes and Results:

Uniformity and Scalability: Stellantis can deploy a single, consistent platform across all its brands, simplifying development and maintenance.

Supply Chain Resilience: Working directly with Qualcomm on a unified platform helps Stellantis better manage and mitigate supply-chain disruptions.

Enhanced User Experience: The solution enables immersive, personalized, and continually upgradeable in-car experiences, meeting modern consumer expectations for connected vehicles.

New Revenue Streams: The Car-to-Cloud platform allows Stellantis to offer subscription-based and on-demand services, opening up new business models. 

Other Notable Deals

BMW Group: BMW selected the Snapdragon Ride platform for its next-generation automated driving and ADAS systems, which will be featured in its "Neue Klasse" electric vehicles.

Renault Group: Qualcomm is collaborating with Renault to co-develop a software-defined vehicle (SDV) architecture, integrating a wide range of functionalities from cockpit to ADAS.

General Motors (GM): GM uses the Snapdragon Cockpit Platform in its Cadillac EVs (e.g., Lyriq, Escalade IQ) for advanced in-cabin experiences and connectivity.
