**DSL (Digital Subscriber Line)** is a broadband internet technology that provides internet access over existing telephone copper wires.

### In 3 lines

- DSL uses the same telephone line used for landline calls.
- It separates voice and internet traffic using a splitter, so both can work simultaneously.
- A **DSL modem** at your home connects to the telephone line and communicates with the ISP's equipment.

### Simple flow

```
Your PC   ↓DSL Modem   ↓Telephone Copper Wire   ↓ISP DSLAM   ↓Internet
```

**DSL vs Cable Modem**

- **DSL:** Uses telephone lines.
- **Cable Modem:** Uses TV coaxial cable lines.
- Cable often offers higher speeds, while DSL speed depends heavily on distance from the ISP's exchange.

Examples of DSL technologies include:

- **ADSL** (Asymmetric DSL)
- **VDSL** (Very-high-bit-rate DSL)

- [ ] ADSL is called _asymmetric_ because download speed is typically much higher than upload speed.
